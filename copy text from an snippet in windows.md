Yes. Windows can do this too, although it's not quite as seamless as macOS's built-in Live Text (depending
on your Windows version). Here are the best options:

Option 1: Windows Snipping Tool (Best Built-in Method)
If you're on Windows 11:
1. Pause the video on the frame you want.
2. Press Win + Shift + S (or open Snipping Tool). you will see an option to like this `[-]` <img width="51" height="41" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/00fae6da-6c60-4fab-830d-45a83d3b5e81" />

3. `Click the Text Actions (OCR) button`.
4. Click Copy all text or select specific text to copy.

This works very well for:
. YouTube videos
. PDFs

. Images
. Any text visible on your screen
